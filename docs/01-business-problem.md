# Business Problem

## Background

Commercial credit applications are typically more complex than retail loan applications. A single case may involve several facilities, collateral items, guarantors, financial statements, policy exceptions, approval authorities, and multiple operational handoffs.

In the current scenario, Relationship Managers submit business credit applications through a Business Credit System. Credit Analysts review the case, Approvers decide the credit outcome, Credit Administration prepares facility and documentation steps, and Compliance may be involved when risk indicators require review.

The system is usable, but several activities still depend on manual follow-up and offline trackers.

## Problem Statement

The Business Credit System does not provide enough structure and visibility across the full credit application journey.

As a result:

- Relationship Managers may submit cases with missing documents or incomplete facility details.
- Credit Analysts spend time clarifying basic information instead of assessing credit quality.
- Approval routing is not always transparent to front-line users.
- Policy exceptions are captured in notes instead of a controlled exception workflow.
- Credit Administration may receive approved cases before all conditions are clearly marked.
- Management cannot easily see where applications are stuck.
- UAT evidence and change request impact may be disconnected from the requirements they validate.
- Stakeholders may need to switch across multiple views to understand one case's lifecycle, blockers, evidence, and next owner.

## Business Impact

| Impact Area | Practical Effect |
| --- | --- |
| Turnaround time | Cases spend time waiting for missing documents, unclear ownership, or manual follow-up. |
| Rework | Credit Analysts return cases for basic corrections that could be validated earlier. |
| Control | Exception approvals and checklist completion may be harder to evidence during review. |
| User experience | RM and Credit teams spend time asking for status updates instead of progressing cases. |
| Reporting | Pipeline and bottleneck reporting depends on offline trackers. |

## Target Outcome

The enhancement should make the process more controlled and easier to manage.

Target outcomes:

- Fewer incomplete submissions
- Clear case ownership and status
- Approval routing based on defined business rules
- Structured exception capture and approval
- Document checklist generated by product and customer type
- Better visibility of pending cases and aging
- UAT coverage aligned to real business scenarios
- Traceable control evidence for waiver, override, exception, and release sign-off decisions
- A case-level lifecycle view that explains readiness posture, blockers, owner actions, and linked evidence

## Success Measures

The figures below are portfolio assumptions, not production claims.

| Metric | Baseline Assumption | Target |
| --- | ---: | ---: |
| Applications returned for missing mandatory documents | 28% | Below 15% |
| Standard renewal cases requiring manual routing correction | 18% | Below 5% |
| Cases without clear next owner after submission | 12% | Below 2% |
| Average time spent in "Pending RM Action" for incomplete cases | 4.5 working days | Below 2.5 working days |
| Monthly credit pipeline report preparation | 1 working day | Same-day dashboard refresh |

## Non-Goals

This project does not attempt to:

- Replace credit judgment with automated approval.
- Define a real bank's credit policy.
- Build a production scoring model.
- Store real customer or borrower information.
- Integrate directly with core banking, CCRIS, document management, or regulatory reporting systems.

## Business Rationale

The enhancement is grounded in a simple banking principle: a credit process should be structured enough to support sound credit granting, administration, monitoring, and control.

Public credit risk and technology risk references emphasize the need for sound credit processes, controls, auditability, and operational discipline. This case study translates those principles into BA artifacts for a commercial credit modernization program.
